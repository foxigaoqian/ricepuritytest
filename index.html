What is the Rice Purity Test?

The Rice Purity Test started back in 1924 at Rice University in Houston, Texas. when a few students made a short survey to see how “pure” people were based on their life experiences. Years later, in the 1980s, Rice students turned it into a bigger 100-question quiz.

This test was primarily created for first-year college students, especially for O-Week (Orientation Week) students, to serve as a private self-assessment quiz.

Its main goal was to give freshmen an enjoyable and private way to reflect on their past life experiences, habits, and relationships before starting college life. Over time, the test has become very popular, and today people of all ages take it, including students, couples, and friends.

The score is calculated based on how many Question’s checkbox you tick. Every box you tick meaning the more experiences you’ve had, the lower your score will be. Once you finish all 100 questions, your final number between 0 and 100 shows your overall level of innocence. A higher number usually means you’re more innocent or haven’t explored much yet. In contrast, a lower score means you’ve had more real-life experiences.

When people ask what is a good rice purity score, it really depends on your age and lifestyle. For most, anything above 80 is seen as “innocent,” while scores under 30 are often viewed as more experienced. But what is a bad rice purity score? Honestly, there isn’t one, every score just tells a different story about who you are and the choices you’ve made.

Origin and History of the Rice Purity Test

Early Campus Origins

The test originated at Rice University in Houston, Texas, where it was distributed informally among freshmen during orientation activities. Its purpose was to encourage conversation and bonding among new students.

Expansion Beyond Rice University

As students shared photocopies and later digital versions, the test spread to other universities across the United States.

Rise of Internet Versions

With the growth of online forums and social media, the Rice Purity Test evolved into a viral internet phenomenon, widely shared on Reddit, TikTok, and group chats.

Why the 100-Question Format Remained

Despite many variations, the original 100-question structure remains the most recognized and trusted version.

How Does the Rice Purity Test Work?

  • You begin with a score of 100.
  • Each checked item subtracts one point.
  • Your final score reflects total life experiences.

Is My Rice Purity Score Normal?

There is no “good” or “bad” Rice Purity score. The test does not measure morality, intelligence, or personal worth.

Scores simply reflect individual life paths and experiences.

Common Misunderstandings

  • A lower score does not mean someone is better.

Is the Rice Purity Test Accurate?

The Rice Purity Test is intended purely for entertainment. It is not a medical, psychological, or diagnostic assessment.
